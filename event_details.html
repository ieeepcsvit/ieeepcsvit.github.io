TOPICS

Education system in India Need for education reforms such as scholarships, availability and quality of teachers, physical education, education infrastructure, selection process, reservation policy in education, privileges of mess and food to rural and poor students, quality of syllabus, quota system, fee exemption, literacy rate, privatisation of educational institutions, grading system etc.

Infrastructural policy of India The state of government buildings in India,their location in the city and the facilities they offer. Industrial infrastructure, urban infrastructure, city planning, work environment, need for modernisation, maintenance policy, rural infrastructure and need for better sanitation etc.

Unemployment and its causes Discussion on quality and quantity of workers, their skills and qualifications, frictional unemployment, seasonal unemployment, India as a cheap labour providing state, lack of work ethics among graduates, overpopulation, lack of jobs, morale of Indian work force, methods of reducing unemployment, voluntary unemployment, role and contribution of rural industries etc.

India’s Diplomatic policy Foreign affairs and foreign capital management, Economic and trade relations, Border problems, India –Pakistan relationship, Indian alliances, India – China relationship, India’s reputation in the world, India and United Nations, Rights of passage, scientific and nuclear trade treaties, Setting up and management of Indian embassies, India – US relationship etc.

Public transportation in India Status of public transport, their need and availability, problem of routes, condition of public transports and their reliability, need for better co-ordination, security issues in public transports, fares, replacement and repairing of older vehicles, need for privatisation in transport sector, cleanliness and maintenance of railway stations and bus stands, disaster management, quality of government officials and drivers, punctuality issues etc.

Human resource management Employee management – recruitment and training. Talent management, prevention of ‘brain drain’, organization development, hierarchy in institutions, overpopulation in india and population control, management of wages and salaries, need for incentives and benefits, provisions for less popular career paths, relocation and re-settlement, migration problems, shortage of residential land, tribal communities and their rights, religious tolerance etc

Budget and financial management Review of tax laws and tax rates, banking policies of government banks, import and export duties, custom laws, state budget and union budget, collection of taxes, allotment of funds, government salaries, effect of inflation on annual budget, money drain, maintenance costs, military expenditure, corruption losses etc.

Entertainment industry Government’s role in entertainment industry, broadcasting services, news services and radio, development of film making industry, import and export of films, anti-piracy policy, censorship, press regulation, regulation of advertisement and television content, funding traditional art and entertainment rituals, copyright management etc

Enforcement of laws Includes but is not limited to Indian Police Service(IPS), Bureaucratic procedures, Right to Information(RTI), strengthening of laws, appointment of judges, Central Bureau of Investigation and other agencies of law enforcement, Women empowerment and women’s rights, Governance of law enforcement agencies, police’s resources, Number of police personnel and their recruitment etc.

Poverty in India Review of the poverty line, malnutrition, child labour, overpopulation, size of family, agricultural productivity, decline of rural industries, privileges for the poor and their misuse, reduction of poverty, unemployment and wage allotment, inflation, caste system, availability of loans and their interest rate, the slums, nationwide hunger etc.
